Recipes for Yummy Chocolate Chip Cookies

This recipe is based off Bon Appetit's recipe.
I made a couple changes to better fit my palette and what I had on hand

Bon Appetit's Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies With Toffee Recipe

Ingredients:

Click to see ingredients!
  • 3/4 cup of unsalted butter
  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp of baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp of salt
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 2 tsps of vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups of chocolate chips

Directions:

Step 1

Preheat oven to 375 degrees Farenheit. Be sure to have the rack in the middle of the oven to evenly distribute heat to the cookies once inside.

Step 2

Cook the 3/4 cup of unsalted butter in a saucepan over medium heat, frequently stir until browning occurs (typically 5-8 minutes). Pour into large bowl and let sit until it's harden.

Step 3

Sift 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 tsp of baking soda, and 1/2 tsp of salt into a medium bowl and combine with a whisk.

Step 4

Add 1 cup of brown sugar and 1/3 granulated sugar to the cooled browned butter. Whisk until combined. Add two large eggs, room temperature, and 2 tsps of vanilla extract. The mixture should start to lighten and thicken.

Step 5

Add the dry ingredients and whisk. Once the dry mixture is incorporated with the wet mixture, add your chocolate chip cookies and mix with a spatula.

Step 6

Line baking sheet with parchment paper. Recommended to use an icecream scoop to keep a consistent cookie size so that they all cook the same. Sprinkle with salt (optional).

Step 7

Bake cookies for 9-11 minutes, until the edges are golden brown. Let cookies cool on the baking sheet for 10 minutes, then transfer to serving plate. Repeat the process with the remaining dough.

My experience and input:

Texture Ratio Consistency Taste Looks
9/10 10/10 6/10 8/10 10/10

When I first made these cookies with my brother, they were an instant hit. I was so happy to have finally found a recipe that satisfied my wants in a cookie. However, I recently made the cookies last year, and they were not as enjoyable as I remember, which is why my rating for consistency is so low. When I had made the cookies, it just seemed incredibly buttery and would not harden. Recently, I have made changes to the recipe to cut down on the amount of butter from 1 cup to 3/4 cup. Additionally, for the actual process of making them, I have decided to let my brown butter completely harden in the fridge instead of doing Bon Appetit's suggestion of just leaving them out til cooled. I think this helps with the consistency a lot more. I opted out of the toffee bits because I never had any laying around, plus I saw reviews saying that they were to hard to bite into. Overall, this recipe is still pretty good, just needed some fine-tuning to my taste!
